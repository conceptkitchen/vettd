### 3. "What if OpenAI just builds this?"

If pushed: "OpenAI's incentive is to make their models permissive and useful. Graded's incentive is to make prompts safe. Those are opposing incentives. That's why third-party security always exists alongside the platform."

### 4. "What about Snyk, Aikido, or other security scanners?"
**"They scan infrastructure. Pipes, configs, APIs, dependencies. We scan content. The actual prompt text. Look at slide 6 — nobody else does shared prompt scanning, trust grading, or auto-learning patterns. We don't compete with them. We complete the stack."**

If pushed: "In fact, we use Aikido and CodeRabbit in our own SDLC. They scan our code. We scan the prompts. Complementary, not competitive."

### 5. "How big is this market?"
**"Prompt security is a subset of AI security, which Gartner projects at $60B+ by 2028. But the real number: there are 260,000 prompts on marketplaces today, millions shared daily, and zero scanning. The market doesn't exist yet because nobody built the product. We did."**

If pushed: "The SSL certificate market is $4.2B. 'Graded Verified' is the same model applied to AI prompts. Marketplaces will license the badge the same way websites license the padlock."

---

### 6. "What's your go-to-market?"
**"Three channels. Free scanner drives awareness and grows the pattern library. Pro API captures developers who want to scan at scale. Graded Verified captures marketplaces who want trust badges. Free → paid → enterprise. Classic PLG."**

---

### 7. "What's stopping someone from copying this?"
**"The pattern library. 182+ patterns today, growing with every scan. The AI deep scan learns new techniques, validates them, and persists them to a Neon Postgres database permanently. That's a data network effect. Plus we auto-sync from three open source repos: Augustus, CyberAlb, and CL4R1T4S. A copycat starts at zero. We're already compounding. First mover with a growing dataset is the hardest thing to replicate in security."**

---

### 8. "Why is this a venture-scale business and not a feature?"
**"Features get absorbed. Platforms don't. Graded isn't a scan button — it's a trust layer. The badge, the API, the pattern library, the marketplace integrations. That's infrastructure. Norton didn't stay a feature inside Windows. Cloudflare didn't stay a feature inside AWS. Independent security layers become platforms."**

### 10. "What's the hardest problem you haven't solved yet?"
**"False positives. A self-learning scanner that flags too aggressively loses trust. That's why the grading system exists — it's not pass/fail, it's a spectrum. An A isn't perfect, an F isn't unusable. The grade gives users context instead of a binary block. But calibrating that at scale is the technical challenge."**

## Cass's Rules for Q&A

1. **Short answers win.** 2 minutes = 2-3 questions max. Don't ramble. Hit the bold line and stop. Silence after a good answer is confidence.
2. **"I don't know" is fine.** If they ask something you don't know, say "That's a great question, I'd love to dig into that with you after. Can I get your card?" Turns a gap into a follow-up meeting.
3. **Always redirect to the product.** Every answer should end near "and you can try it right now." That's your unfair advantage. Use it.
4. **Don't defend. Advance.** If they challenge, don't get defensive. Acknowledge and pivot: "That's fair. Here's how we're thinking about it."
5. **The last thing you say is what they remember.** End every answer on strength, not caveat.
